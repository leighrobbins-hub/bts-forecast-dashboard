Methodology Documentation

Version 4.0 | Last Updated: -

Overview & Purpose

This analysis compares back-to-school forecast demand (April–October 2026) against organic recruiting capacity using a three-signal classification system: new tutor placement rate, all-tutor hours utilization, and P90 time-to-assign. The combination pinpoints whether a subject needs recruiting, placement investigation, forecast reduction, or no action at all.

Key Innovation: Multi-signal classification replaces the earlier single-metric approach. Each subject receives a Primary Action (one of eight categories) plus zero or more Stress Flags that can stack independently. This separates "what to do" from "what to watch," enabling more targeted weekly decisions.

Data Sources & Quality

1. Run Rate Baseline (Dec 2025–Mar 2026)

Source: Looker Look for new tutor contracting by subject by month.

Calculation: Run_Rate = Mean(Dec 2025, Jan 2026, Feb 2026, Mar 2026)

Why Dec–Mar 2026 Only?
  • Excludes Nov 2025: Manual subject adds inflated supply through January 2026.
  • Excludes Pre-Dec 2025: Paid-spend campaigns inflated run rates above organic baseline.
  • TVA Stabilization: Late 2025 process change (HireVue → Micro1 → TVA) reduced acceptance rates from ~50% to ~15%. Dec 2025 forward = post-transition baseline.
  • Strict SOP: February 2026 implemented stricter subject approval.

2. Forecast Data (V1.4 Model)

Source: monitoring_table_wide.xlsx (Pierre’s forecast model).

Metric: forecasted_headcount — predicted new tutor contracting need by subject by month.

3. New Tutor 30-Day Placement Rate

Source: Looker Look for tutor assignment timing.

Metric: Of tutors newly contracted, what percentage received any assignment within 30 days. Displayed in the UI as New Tutor 30d % (formerly “Util %”).

Interpretation: Below 30% signals new tutors aren’t being absorbed into active work — a placement or algorithm issue.

4. All Tutor Hours Utilization

Source: Looker Look (LOOKER_TUTOR_HOURS_UTIL_LOOK_ID environment variable). Written to data/tutor_hours_util.csv.

Metric: Across ALL tutors on a subject (not just newly contracted ones), actual hours worked divided by the hours tutors said they wanted. Above 100% means tutors are carrying more load than requested; below 100% means idle capacity exists.

Thresholds used in classification:

  • ≥ 115% → pool is overworked (“cracking”)
  • < 60% → substantial idle capacity exists
  • > 120% → burnout_risk stress flag
  • < 50% → idle_pool stress flag

5. P90 Time-to-Assign (NAT Hours)

Source: Looker Look 26319. Written to data/nat_p90.csv.

Metric: The 90th-percentile time (in hours) from a student requesting a placement to being matched with a tutor. The worst 10% of placements wait at least this long.

Tiered goals by subject volume:

A compressed snapshot of all dashboard data (summary, subjects, tracker, recommendations) is written daily by run_analysis.py. Up to 30 days are retained. This enables week-over-week comparisons in the WBR tab.

Analysis Steps

Step 1: Demand Adjustment (3-Month Group Smoothing)

Problem: Original forecasts show volatile month-to-month demand. Tutors recruited too early are likely to attrit before needed.

Solution: Smooth demand within fixed 3-month groups (Aug/Sep/Oct, May/Jun/Jul, standalone Apr). If any month in a group exceeds the run-rate cap, the group total is averaged across its three months. Manual floors are always respected.

Example — SAT (Run Rate: 11/mo):

Month Original Smoothed Change
Apr2728+1
May2630+4
Jun3032+2
Jul3630-6
Aug4244+2
Sep4545—
Oct4644-2
Total252253Demand preserved within groups

Group 1 (Aug/Sep/Oct): 42+45+46 = 133 → 44, 45, 44. Group 2 (May/Jun/Jul): 26+30+36 = 92 → 30, 32, 30. April left as-is since Group 2 had corrections.

Step 2: Capacity Gap Analysis

Metrics Calculated:

  • Gap = Adjusted_Target - Run_Rate
  • Gap_Pct = (Gap / Run_Rate) × 100
  • Max_Capacity = Run_Rate × 1.2 (20% stretch assumed achievable with focused effort)

Threshold Logic:

  • If Adjusted_Target > Max_Capacity → Flag as "Needs External Levers"
  • If Adjusted_Target ≤ Max_Capacity → "On Track" (achievable with organic pipeline)

Step 3: Multi-Signal Classification (Primary Action + Stress Flags)

Each subject receives exactly one Primary Action (what to do) and zero or more Stress Flags (what to watch). The three input signals are:

  1. New Tutor 30d % — are newly contracted tutors getting assigned?
  2. All Tutor Hours Util % — is the existing pool overworked or idle?
  3. P90 Time-to-Assign — are students waiting too long?

Plus a derived flag: Needs External Levers = target_per_month > run_rate × 1.2 (monthly target exceeds 120% of organic run rate).

Backward Compatibility

A legacy Problem_Type field is still computed from Primary_Action for older code paths. The mapping: Recruit → True Supply Problem, Investigate (Hidden/Capacity) → Under-Used, Wait Times → On Track — High Wait, Reduce Forecast → Over-Supplied, On Track → On Track, Insufficient Data → Supply Problem (No Util Data).

Step 4: Recommendations Engine

Each subject’s Primary Action drives a specific recommendation with a detailed reason string citing the actual per-row metrics:

  • Recruit More / Urgent: Cites run rate gap and hours util. Recommends deploying recruiting levers.
  • Recruit More (paper supply): Low hours util but P90 proves current supply isn’t converting to real placements. Recommends recruiting active tutors.
  • Investigate — Hidden Supply: Cites both hours util and placement rate. Asks team to investigate before recruiting.
  • Investigate — Capacity Available: Cites low hours util despite supply gap, but P90 within goal confirms genuine idle capacity. Asks whether capacity can be unlocked first.
  • Investigate — Wait Times: Cites P90 vs tiered goal. Asks to examine assignment flow.
  • Reduce Forecast: Cites all three metrics. Recommends considering forecast reduction.
  • Behind-pace alerts are layered on top for in-progress months using calendar-prorated targets.

Key Findings

Findings are dynamic — the dashboard hero board and WBR show current counts by Primary Action category. Refer to the Overview tab for the latest distribution across Recruit, Investigate, On Track, Wait Times, and Reduce Forecast buckets.

Limitations & Caveats

Run Rate Volatility (High Concern)

Month-to-month capacity swings significantly:

SubjectDecJanFebMarMeanStd DevCV
SAT87141511.03.532%
High School Chemistry241171414.06.345%
AP Pre-Calculus63395.22.547%

Implication: Run rate is NOT a stable capacity baseline - it's a volatile trailing indicator. Using point estimates (single average) without confidence bands masks significant uncertainty.

Recommended Enhancement: Add confidence intervals (e.g., Run_Rate ± 1 Std Dev) to capacity estimates. Flag subjects with CV >40% as "high variance - monitor closely."

Forecast Accuracy Unknown

Pierre's V1.4 model has limited historical validation:

  • 4-month backtest (Nov 2025 - Feb 2026) showed good MAE vs. manual forecasts
  • But only 4 months of actuals available (small sample)
  • Model assumes stable demand patterns (back-to-school surge may differ from historical)
  • Existing capacity feature pending (may materially change forecasts)

Mitigation: Track actual Apr 2026 contracting vs. forecast as early validation. Adjust methodology if significant over/under forecasting detected.

Acceptance Rate Structural Constraint

Critical Context: Funnel Broken

Late 2025 contracting process change reduced acceptance rates from ~50% to ~15%. This is a 70% decline, requiring 3.3x more applications to achieve same contracted volume.

Impact: To contract 36 SAT tutors/month:

  • At 50% acceptance: need 72 applications
  • At 15% acceptance: need 240 applications (3.3x more!)

Implication: Even with paid spend increasing application volume, 15% conversion constrains scalability. Funnel optimization may be higher ROI than recruitment spend.

Recommended Parallel Workstream: Track acceptance rate monthly (Dec-Mar 2026 trend analysis). If declining further, TVA process fixes become higher priority than forecast analysis.

Validation Against Prior Work

LSAT Utilization Investigation (Completed Week 11, 2026)

Findings validated by current analysis:

  • LSAT showed 22% new tutor utilization vs. 66% veteran utilization
  • Current analysis: LSAT utilization 33% (Feb-Mar 2026) - consistent with investigation
  • Root causes: Four-stage exclusion cascade, capacity misdirection, ranking issues
  • Conclusion: LSAT is utilization-constrained, not supply-constrained

Current analysis extends this finding: 24 subjects exhibit same pattern (low utilization + forecast gap). Recommendation: Apply LSAT investigation methodology to Chemistry and College Accounting as highest-volume representatives.

Stockout Analysis (Week 8, 2026)

Found 69% of stockouts were process-related, not supply-related. This aligns with utilization problem hypothesis - system barriers preventing tutor-student matches even when supply exists.

V1.4 Forecast Accuracy Backtest

Model performed well against manual forecasts (31% MAE improvement), providing confidence in forecast quality. However, historical accuracy doesn't guarantee future accuracy - especially for back-to-school surge pattern.

Assumptions & Constraints

Key Assumptions:

  1. 120% capacity threshold: Assumes recruiting can stretch to 20% above average with focused effort but not beyond
  2. Even distribution optimal: Assumes consistent monthly recruitment is operationally superior to reactive ramping
  3. 30-day utilization standard: Tutors should receive first assignment within 30 days (longer indicates system issue)
  4. Dec-Mar baseline stable: Assumes last 4 months represent go-forward capacity (may not account for seasonal patterns)
  5. Utilization patterns persistent: Assumes Feb-Mar utilization rates representative of ongoing patterns

Known Constraints:

  • Cannot model seasonality: Dec-Mar may not represent summer/fall recruiting capacity (different from spring)
  • Cannot predict SOP changes: Future policy shifts could alter run rates significantly
  • Cannot predict acceptance rate: If TVA acceptance continues declining, capacity will worsen
  • Cannot predict demand shifts: Back-to-school patterns may differ from historical (exam changes, curriculum updates, etc.)

Recommended Use & Interpretation

Primary Use Cases

  1. WBR Preparation: Review the Weekly Summary tab for the six-section WBR report covering headline, pace, movers, behind-pace subjects, zero-velocity watch list, and decisions narrative.
  2. Resource Allocation: Use Primary Action to decide whether to recruit, investigate, reduce forecast, or take no action.
  3. Risk Monitoring: Stress Flags (shown as colored pills) highlight subjects that need watching even if their Primary Action is benign.
  4. Trend Monitoring: Week-over-week comparisons powered by daily snapshots.

Interpretation by Primary Action

  • Recruit More / Urgent: Deploy recruiting levers. Gap is real and pipeline is the bottleneck.
  • Recruit More (paper supply): Low hours util looks idle, but P90 wait times prove the “capacity” is illusory. Existing roster isn’t converting — recruit active tutors.
  • Investigate — Hidden Supply / Capacity Available: Do NOT blindly recruit. Capacity may already exist — investigate placement, algorithm, or scheduling first.
  • Investigate — Wait Times: Students waiting too long. Examine matching flow, credential checks, scheduling.
  • Reduce Forecast: Demand may be overestimated. All signals show surplus. Consider lowering the target.
  • On Track: No action needed. Monitor stress flags for early warning.
  • Insufficient Data: Cannot classify. Prioritize getting data for this subject.

Stress Flag Guidance

  • burnout_risk (red): Tutors carrying > 120% of desired hours. May lead to attrition.
  • idle_pool (blue): Existing tutors have substantial unused capacity. Check if routing is efficient.
  • new_tutor_stuck (orange): < 30% of new tutors placed. Likely a placement/algorithm issue.
  • critical_wait / high_wait (red/amber): P90 exceeds 2× or 1.5× the tier goal. Students experience frustrating delays.
  • paper_supply (purple): Supply gap + low hours util + P90 above goal. The “idle capacity” is paper supply — tutors on the roster who aren’t actually handling demand. Recruit active tutors.
  • healthy_p90_override (green): A per-subject P90 goal override is active.

Weekly Business Review (WBR)

The Weekly Summary tab generates a six-section WBR report designed for copy-paste into documents or presentations:

  1. Headline: Auto-generated 3-sentence summary with contracting %, pace-vs-target delta, WoW combos added, and biggest concern.
  2. Pace & Momentum: Table with This Week / Last Week / Δ / Status columns for key KPIs. Conditional cell coloring.
  3. Biggest Movers: Positive framing — subjects that moved into on-track, velocity leaders.
  4. Behind Pace — Top 10: Subjects sorted ascending by % of monthly target. Includes combos remaining and days-to-close at current velocity.
  5. Zero-Velocity Watch List: Top 10–15 subjects with zero contracting in the review period.
  6. Decisions Activity: Narrative summary of decisions logged during the last complete Sunday–Saturday week, synthesizing notes, action types, and recommendations.

WoW comparisons use daily snapshots. The review window is the last complete Sunday–Saturday week, displayed explicitly (e.g., “Sun, Apr 12 – Sat, Apr 18”).

Decision Tracking

Each recommended action can be marked with a decision (Action or No Action) via a dropdown in the Subjects & Actions tab. Decisions include who decided, when, and freeform notes. Data is persisted via Netlify Blobs and synced across all signed-in users.

Monthly Tracker

The Monthly Tracker tab shows per-subject, per-month progress. Targets are calendar-prorated: on day 22 of a 30-day month, the expected pace is 22/30 = 73.3% of the monthly goal. Subjects are flagged as behind-pace (< 80%), at-risk (80–99%), or on-pace (≥ 100%). A March baseline column shows actual vs. forecast for the pre-BTS month.
